Reference: Anglia, N. (2020) (Accessed: November 1, 2022)

What is Computational Thinking?

 In this fast paced world, we are constantly facing new problem in our daily life. A complex problem would be difficult to crack and we cannot use the tools that we normally use to help solve the problem. Without a proper strategy, it certainly would be difficult and possibly make it even harder to solve. A common process of solving complex problems is known as computational thinking. It is the thought processes involved in solving a problem and developing its solution by applying ideas, concepts and methods in such a way that a computer, human or machine can effectively carry it out.

Key Concepts for Computational Thinking

Computational thinking involves four steps such as below:

• Decomposition: Decomposition is the first step of computational thinking process. Decomposition involves breaking a complex problem down into smaller and more manageable parts. By dividing the problem into smaller parts, it allows us to understand the problem better and develop effective solutions to tackle each part of the problem.

• Pattern recognition: Second step of computational thinking process is pattern recognition. Pattern recognition is simply identifying patterns or trends within a problem that we might have encountered in the past and then apply what we have learned from it that may help us to develop an effective solution.

• Abstraction: Step 3 of computational thinking process is abstraction. A complex problem is divided into smaller parts can be classified by identifying the similar details that are related to the problem and disregarding unrelated information without losing the important detail to work toward a solution. This is usually the hardest part of computational thinking.

• Algorithm Design: The final step of computational thinking process is algorithm design. Algorithm design involves constructing the flowchart and developing step-by-step instruction to solve the problem. The method can be used again to solve similar problem.

Why is Computational Thinking Important?

  In today’s world, computational thinking influences every aspect of a modern business. Most of the business involve in collecting and analyzing data such as evaluating consumer’s behavior, storing individual health data or tracking financial markets movements. A lot of jobs require problem solver to make improvements to increase the efficiency of a business or making innovation products and services for consumers. (Snalune.P, 2020)

 Computational thinking skills are useful and valuable to jobs in every sector, including banking and investment firms, fast moving consumer goods, computer and software services, travel and leisure industry, energy and manufacturing industry or public services such as healthcare, transportation, education and law and regulatory. Employees from respective field are required to demonstrate their computational thinking skills to actively creating and solving problems. Those relevant skills can be applied to any role in public or private sector and commercial business. (Cummins.K, 2022) For instances, computational thinking skills can even be applied on daily life such as cooking. Starting with decomposing cooking process into multiple steps such as ingredients preparation, buying, washing and cutting. Followed by pattern recognition and abstraction by categorizing the type of ingredients can or cannot be used for different dishes. The final step would be cooking step-by-step.

  Computational thinking can be applied on some real-world problems as well. For instances, weather forecasting is based on the past year weather history using patterns of generalization or abstraction. Retail e-commerce site captures user journey to promote relevant information to the users. This involves using decomposition techniques, dividing a problem down into its smaller parts and formulating a strategic way to solve the problem using algorithmic thinking.

  With computational thinking concept, we are able to come out with the solution to the problem that may previously have seemed impossible to solve. It is a very efficient and effective skill to equip and it will benefit us for life.
